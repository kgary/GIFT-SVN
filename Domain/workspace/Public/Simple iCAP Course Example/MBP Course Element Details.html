This course is meant to showcase iCAP and Adaptive Courseflow branching in GIFT as well as provide a simple means to test functionality.

There are two Adaptive Courseflow course objects.

‘some concept B’

The first covers the concept named ‘some concept B’ (e.g. ‘map reading’).  This is meant to be simple.  First you will be given a pretest that covers this concept.  If you are deemed an expert on this concept you will skip the Rule, Example and Recall quadrants and directly start Practicing.  Here are the quadrants broken down:

·         Rule – there are 2 PowerPoint shows that GIFT will choose from.

·         Example – there are 2 PowerPoint shows that GIFT will choose from.

·         Recall – 3 questions tagged with ‘some concept B’ will be chosen from the question bank

·         Practice –  there is 1 PowerPoint show that GIFT will choose from.

‘some concept A’ and ‘some concept C’

The second covers the concepts named ‘some concept A’ and ‘some concept C’.  This is meant to be a more complex example of what can be authored in GIFT.  First you will be given a pretest that covers those two concepts.  If you are deemed an expert on both you will skip the Rule, Example and Recall quadrants and directly start Practicing.  Here are the quadrants broken down:

·         Rule –

o   A+C –

There are 2 PowerPoint shows that GIFT will choose from.

o   A – There is 1 PowerPoint show that GIFT will choose from.

o   C – There is 1 webpage that GIFT will choose from.

 

·         Example –

o   A+C – there are 2 PowerPoint shows to choose from.

·         Recall – 3 questions for each concept will be chosen from the question bank.

·         Practice – there are 2 PowerPoint shows that GIFT will choose from.
